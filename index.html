Abstract

My_OrcaHand++ is an improved OrcaHand project based on Feetech servos. Its core functions include joint limit position and maximum current calibration, motion choreography, and gesture-guided grasping. The project is mainly implemented by adapting the original control interface to the corresponding packaged Feetech servo APIs and combining it with MediaPipe hand landmark recognition, with the core functions integrated into a unified host-computer UI.

The project implements two key calibration procedures. For joint range calibration, each joint is traversed from the neutral position of 2048 toward its maximum position with a step size of +5, saved by keyboard confirmation, and then traversed toward its minimum position with a step size of -5 before moving to the next joint. For current calibration, the hand repeatedly performs five fist-open cycles, because the fist motion produces the largest joint current; the measured maximum currents are written back to the configuration file to reduce unnecessary over-current protection triggers.

The upper-computer interface includes demonstrations such as finger traversal, forward and backward waving, left and right waving, fist closing, and thumb-to-finger traversal. For gesture recognition and gesture-guided grasping, the system uses MediaPipe Hands to estimate 21 hand landmarks, defines the palm plane and base coordinate frame, maps the landmarks to 17 robotic hand joints, and converts joint angles into calibrated servo ranges. A weighted mapping fuses MCP, PIP, and DIP flexion into two servo commands, while an exponential moving average smooths the motion during execution.

Maximum Position & Current Calibration

The calibration workflow records reliable joint limits and current thresholds before motion execution. Joint extrema are saved by stepping each servo from the neutral position to its maximum and minimum positions, while current calibration captures peak current during repeated fist-open cycles.

Programmed Motions

The upper-computer interface includes a set of reusable demonstration motions for testing joint coordination, tendon routing, servo response, and hand-level behavior.

Gesture-Guided Grasping

MediaPipe Hands estimates human hand landmarks that are mapped to the robotic hand joints. Weighted MCP/PIP/DIP fusion and exponential moving average smoothing allow the hand to follow gestures while producing stable grasping motions.
